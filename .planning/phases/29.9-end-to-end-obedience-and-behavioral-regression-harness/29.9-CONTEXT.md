# Phase 29.9 Context

## problem statement

Packet health and storage truth are not enough.

The product only wins if actual ordinary answers obey the active behavior policy across realistic prompts.

## why this phase exists

This phase converts the strongest valid criticism into a product gate:

- not "can Brainstack store or recall something"
- but "does the assistant actually behave correctly"

## accepted evidence entering this phase

- existing tests heavily protect retrieval/plumbing invariants
- they do not yet prove ordinary-turn obedience on real answers strongly enough

## explicit non-goals

- no benchmark-shaped overfitting
- no brittle output regexes as the primary architecture
## recommended model level

`high`
