# Phase 35 Implementation Contract

## invariant

Brainstack must become stronger at operating truth without exploding into a broad ontology, benchmark hack stack, or duplicate owner system.

## required implementation properties

- the operating-truth layer must be small, explicit, and first-class
- continuity summaries may support operating context, but should not remain the main substitute for operating truth
- retrieval should move closer to owner-first logic:
  - identify likely authoritative owner first
  - then apply budget shaping
- owner-first must mean more than a renamed cue list:
  - surface cues may guide ranking and budget decisions
  - they must not remain the primary gate that decides which truth owner is ignored
- typed evidence expansion must stay narrow, honest, and directly tied to operating value
- truth classes should integrate more coherently without losing clear ownership boundaries
- the phase must remain donor-first and update-friendly
- external-owner pointers must become representable and renderable when grounded in committed truth
- operating truth must improve the system’s answer to:
  - what is active now
  - what is still open
  - what is committed
  - what should happen next

## prohibited outcomes

- broad ontology or KG expansion for its own sake
- another behavior-centric detour
- new cue farms as the main owner-selection mechanism
- silent owner ambiguity between operating truth, continuity, graph truth, and transcript evidence
- fake product wins that only improve one narrow phrasing pattern
- replacing explicit operating records with nicer prose summaries and calling it maturity

## likely implementation seams

- `brainstack/operating_context.py`
- `brainstack/control_plane.py`
- `brainstack/executive_retrieval.py`
- `brainstack/retrieval.py`
- `brainstack/graph_evidence.py`
- `brainstack/__init__.py`
- `brainstack/db.py`
- any narrow new operating-truth storage/render seam needed to keep ownership explicit
- any operating-record model or render seam needed to keep active work, decisions, commitments, next steps, and owner pointers first-class without broad ontology growth

## verify contract

- prove that first-class operating records exist and render correctly
- prove that owner-first retrieval reduces query-shape brittleness
- prove that the system is better at surfacing current work / commitments / open decisions
- prove that explicit operating truth outranks continuity-derived fallback when both are present
- prove that the new substrate improves second-brain usefulness rather than merely generating richer operating prose
- prove parity on:
  - source
  - `hermes-final`
  - rebuilt live runtime
