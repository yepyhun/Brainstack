# Phase 63.1 Hard Gates

## 1. canonical-policy gate

- explicit durable user/project rules must have a canonical Brainstack-owned authority surface

## 2. non-governor gate

- the phase must not move scheduler, executor, or approval-governor behavior into Brainstack

## 3. no-save-everything gate

- the design must not solve importance by indiscriminate retention or by treating all transcript residue as durable authority

## 4. plane-separation gate

- skills, durable policy, operating/live-state, transcript evidence, and native compatibility memory must have distinct roles

## 4.1 no-overdesign gate

- the phase must not explode into a many-plane abstraction model when a simpler canonical authority model is enough
- the final design should stay practically implementable:
  - `canonical policy`
  - `operating/live state`
  - `evidence`
  - with `skill` and `native memory` as supporting stores, not equal-authority policy planes

## 5. no-heuristic-importance gate

- the phase must not introduce cue lists, keyword scoring, locale dictionaries, or renamed heuristic ranking to decide what becomes canonical policy

## 6. evidence-not-authority gate

- transcript and continuity may remain supporting evidence
- they must not remain the only durable authority for important explicit rules

## 7. no-skill-only-authority gate

- procedural skills may support behavior, but they must not be the only canonical home for durable user/project doctrine

## 8. runtime-boundary gate

- this phase may define a runtime-facing read snapshot
- it must not implement runtime intake, cron behavior, approval enforcement, or execution

## 9. multimodal gate

- the canonical policy design must remain valid as typed state for future multimodal or non-text event sources

## 10. truth-first closeout gate

- final closeout must say plainly:
  - what is now canonical policy
  - what remains supporting evidence
  - what runtime may later consume
  - what Brainstack still does not own
